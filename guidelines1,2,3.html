Food safety is a critical aspect of public health that ensures the food we consume is safe and free from contaminants. It involves proper handling, storage, and preparation practices to prevent foodborne illnesses. Adhering to food safety guidelines helps protect individuals from harmful bacteria, viruses, and toxins that can cause serious health issues. It also promotes trust in the food supply chain, ensuring consumers feel confident about the food they eat. Overall, food safety is essential for maintaining a healthy community and preventing disease outbreaks.
