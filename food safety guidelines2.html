FOOD SAFETY GUIDELINES


For Food Handlers


  1. Personal Hygiene

    • Maintain clean uniforms and aprons.
    • Use hairnets, gloves, and masks as needed.
    • Avoid handling food when ill.


  2. Hand Washing

    • Wash hands thoroughly before starting work and after handling raw food.
    • Use hand sanitizers only as a supplement to proper handwashing.


  3. Cross-Contamination Prevention

    • Use separate equipment and utensils for different types of food.
    • Clean and sanitize surfaces and equipment after each use.
    • Store chemicals and cleaning agents away from food areas.


  4. Temperature Control

    • Monitor food temperatures regularly.
    • Use calibrated thermometers to check food temperatures.
    • Keep cold food at 40°F or below and hot food at 140°F or above.


  5. Cleaning and Sanitizing

    • Follow a regular schedule for cleaning and sanitizing all surfaces.
    • Use appropriate cleaning agents and follow manufacturer's instructions.
    • Ensure all equipment and utensils are properly sanitized before use.


  6. Receiving and Storing Supplies

    • Inspect deliveries for signs of contamination or damage.
    • Store supplies in a clean, dry place, off the floor, and away from walls.
    • Rotate stock using the FIFO (First In, First Out) method.
