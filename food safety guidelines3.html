FOOD SAFETY GUIDELINES


For Business


  1. Food Safety Management System

    • Implement a HACCP (Hazard Analysis Critical Control Points) plan.
    • Regularly review and update the food safety management system.
    • Train staff on food safety policies and procedures.


  2. Facility Cleanliness

    • Maintain high standards of cleanliness in all areas.
    • Schedule regular deep cleaning and pest control services.
    • Ensure waste is properly disposed of and not accumulating.


  3. Equipment Maintenance

    • Regularly inspect and maintain all equipment.
    • Keep records of maintenance and repairs.
    • Calibrate thermometers and other measuring devices regularly.


  4. Staff Training

    • Provide ongoing food safety training for all employees.
    • Ensure new employees are trained before handling food.
    • Keep training records and certifications up to date.


  5. Compliance and Audits

    • Stay informed about local, national, and international food safety regulations.
    • Prepare for and conduct regular internal and external audits.
    • Address any non-compliance issues promptly and effectively.


  6. Customer Communication

    • Clearly display food safety certifications and inspection results.
    • Provide information on allergen management and ingredient sourcing.
    • Handle customer complaints and feedback regarding food safety promptly.
