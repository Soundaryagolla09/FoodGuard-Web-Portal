FOOD SAFETY GUIDELINES



For Consumers


  1. Purchasing Food

    • Check expiration dates and condition of packaging.
    • Avoid buying damaged or bulging cans.
    • Purchase perishable items last and keep them cold on the way home.


  2. Storing Food

    • Refrigerate perishable food within 2 hours.
    • Keep refrigerator at or below 40°F (4°C) and freezer at 0°F (-18°C).
    • Store raw meat, poultry, and seafood separately from ready-to-eat foods.


  3. Preparing Food

    • Wash hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds before and after handling food.
    • Use separate cutting boards for raw meat and vegetables.
    • Wash fruits and vegetables under running water before eating or cooking.


  4. Cooking Food

    • Cook food to the right temperature: use a food thermometer.
    • Reheat leftovers to at least 165°F (74°C).
    • Follow cooking times and temperatures for different types of food.


  5. Serving Food

    • Keep hot food hot (140°F or above) and cold food cold (40°F or below).
    • Use clean utensils and dishes to serve food.
    • Avoid leaving perishable food out for more than 2 hours (1 hour if above 90°F).


  6. Handling Leftovers

    • Cool leftovers quickly and store them in shallow containers.
    • Consume leftovers within 3-4 days or freeze them.
    • Reheat leftovers to at least 165°F (74°C) before consuming.
